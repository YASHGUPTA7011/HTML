WATER POLLUTION

1.
**Definition**: Water pollution refers to the contamination of water bodies, such as rivers, lakes, and oceans, with harmful substances. 2. **Causes**: Major sources include industrial discharges, agricultural runoff (pesticides and fertilizers), sewage, plastic waste, and oil spills.

3. **Effects on Health**: Contaminated water can cause serious health issues, including gastrointestinal diseases, reproductive problems, and neurological disorders.

4. **Ecosystem Impact**: Polluted water harms aquatic life, disrupting ecosystems, reducing biodiversity, and leading to the death of species.

5. **Economic Consequences**: Water pollution can negatively affect industries like fishing and tourism, leading to economic losses.

6. **Prevention Strategies**: Solutions include improving waste management, regulating pollutants, promoting sustainable agricultural practices, and increasing public awareness.

7. **Global Issue**: Water pollution is a worldwide problem, affecting both developed and developing countries, and requires international cooperation for effective solutions.
